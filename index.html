02 — Target Market

Who to
Target

The ideal client is a local service business — one that generates most of its revenue over the phone and cannot afford to miss a single call.

These are business owners on job sites, under sinks, and on rooftops — not sitting at a desk. Every missed call is a job they did not get. When you show them how many calls they miss each week, the conversation practically closes itself.

Painters
Always on site, rarely near a phone. High-ticket jobs, high cost per missed call.
Plumbers & HVAC

Electricians
High average job value. Missed calls carry outsized revenue impact.
Roofers
Seasonal urgency. One missed storm-damage call can be a $15K loss.
Dentists & Chiro
High appointment volume. Every missed booking is revenue gone.

03 — The System

Your 30-Day
Roadmap

Four weeks. Four phases. Each builds on the last. Do not skip ahead.

01 Week One
Position Yourself
  • Choose one niche — commit to it, no splitting focus
  • Research your market: what do these businesses consistently struggle with
  • Set up a clean outreach presence on Instagram or LinkedIn
  • Write one sharp value proposition for your niche
  • Study three businesses as if you were pitching them tomorrow
Know exactly who you serve and why they need you
02 Week Two
Build Your List
  • Find 10 target businesses via Google Maps, Instagram, or local directories
  • Find the name of the owner or decision maker for each
  • Locate their preferred contact: DM, email, or direct phone
  • Build a simple tracking spreadsheet — name, business, contact, status
  • Qualify each: active presence, recent reviews, signs of growth
10 qualified prospects ready to contact
03 Week Three
Start Conversations
  • Send a personalized message to all 10 — reference something specific to their business
  • Open with curiosity, not a pitch — ask about them first
  • Follow up once with anyone who does not respond within 3 days
  • Aim for at least two discovery calls booked for Week 4
  • Adjust messaging based on which approaches generate real responses
Two or more discovery calls booked
04 Week Four
Close
  • Run your discovery calls — listen far more than you present
  • Ask: how many calls do you miss? What does a missed job cost you?
  • Only present your solution after you fully understand their pain
  • When objections come, find the real concern — it is rarely the price
  • Ask for the decision before the call ends — do not leave it open
Your first signed client
04 — The Close

The Discovery
Call

The sale is made or lost in the first fifteen minutes — not because of your pitch, but because of your questions.

Most people lose deals not because of price but because they never uncovered what the prospect actually cared about. Ask these five questions in order. Let the answers do the selling.

01 /
How do most of your new clients find you?
This establishes how dependent their business is on inbound calls. It frames every missed call as a missed revenue opportunity before you say another word.
02 /
What happens when you miss a call while on the job?
You are asking them to say the pain out loud. A prospect who verbalizes their problem is far more likely to act on it. Let them explain it — do not explain it for them.
03 /
Do you have anyone dedicated to answering calls right now?
This surfaces the gap clearly. Most of the time the answer is no — and that answer alone is worth more than any pitch you could give.
04 /
What would one additional job per week mean for your business?
Now they are doing the math themselves. You are not telling them the value — they are calculating it. Let the silence sit after this one.
05 /
If you never had to worry about a missed call again — what would that be worth?
They are now pricing the solution for you. Whatever number they arrive at, your $5K–$10K fee becomes the obvious move.

The best closers are not the best talkers. They are the best listeners.

Objections
When They Say It's Too Expensive
  • They do not understand the value — walk back through the math they did themselves
  • They do not trust that it works — introduce proof, case studies, or a risk reversal
  • They are not confident enough yet — slow down and keep asking questions
  • Never drop your price before you know which one of these it actually is
